GST (Goods and Services Tax): Understand, Calculate, and Usage of GST Worldwide

​

Most peoples searches for terms like GST, GST tax, GST meaning, or what is indirect taxes. Goods and Services Tax — which is commonly abbreviated as GST — is one of the most widely discussed and searched tax systems in the world. if you're a business owner, a consumer, or a student of economic studies, understanding this tax is essential to navigating modern financial systems.

​

At its core, GST meaning is straightforward: it is a value-added, multi-stage, destination-based tax levied on the supply of goods and services. It is collected at every point of sale, but the final burden of the tax falls on the end consumer. This structure of tax system makes it a consumption tax — you pay it when you use or buy something else, not only when you earn money.

​

In simple language, Goods and Services Tax has been replaced a complicated web of older and indirect taxes like sales tax, service tax, excise duty, and VAT (Value Added Tax). By unifying these under one umbrella, this GST aims to simplify the tax system, eliminate the cascading "tax on tax" effect, and create a seamless national or regional market.

​

This structure of this indirect tax system makes it a consumption tax — you pay it when you use or buy something else, not only when you earn money.

​

The main question around GST is what is goods and services tax has different answers. these answers are depending on where you live in the world, because indirect taxes operates differently across countries on earth. Though the fundamental and financial principles remain the same.

​

GST in India was introduced on 1st July, 2017, as one of the most transformative tax reforms in our country's history. It has replaced over 17 central and state taxes with a unified structure. India's indirect taxes framework runs on four rate slabs: 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%, with essential goods like food grains taxed at 0%. The Goods and Services Tax in India operates on a dual model, with both the central government's tax (CGST) and state governments' taxes (SGST) collecting their respective portions.

​

This tax system in Canada is a federal tax of 5% (nearly) levied on most goods and services. Canada also has a Harmonised Sales Tax (HST) in certain provinces, which combines the federal consumption tax with provincial sales tax. So when people search for GST tax Canada or goods and services tax Canada, they are typically referring to this federal 5% levy, which businesses must collect and remit to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).

​

This tax system in Australia stands at a flat rate of 10% (something) applied to most goods and services sold or consumed within Australia. The Australian GST calculator is a popular search term among both individuals and businesses, as correctly calculating indirect taxes is essential for compliance with the Australian Taxation Office (ATO)

GST and TAXES: how these works-

​

When people in our country look up to GST and tax or tax and GST, they often want to understand how consumption taxes fit within the broader tax landscape. Unlike income tax, which is applied to their monthly or annual income, which is a direct tax on their income but GST is an indirect tax — it is collected by sellers on behalf of the government. Here's a simplified breakdown of how it works:

​
  1. Manufacturer pays to this tax system on raw materials but can claim input tax credit (ITC).
  2. The wholesaler pays indirect taxes on purchase from manufacturer, claims ITC on what was already paid.
  3. The retailer pays GST on the purchase from the wholesaler and again claims ITC.
  4. Consumer pays consumption taxes on the final purchase — and this is the tax that actually gets collected by the government.
​

Note: This input tax credit mechanism is what prevents the cascading effect and ensures that indirect tax for tax purposes is fair, transparent, and efficient.

​

An important element of any taxes discussion is understanding GST tax exemptions and goods and services tax exemptions. Not everything is subject to indirect taxes. Governments typically exempt essential commodities and services to reduce the burden on lower-income populations.

​

In India, fresh fruits, vegetables, milk, eggs, and educational services are exempted from GST because we live in India, where this tax system is applied to everyone else. In Canada, where this tax is also applied on a normal man's pocket. For basic groceries, prescription drugs, and medical devices are indirect taxes-free. In Australia, fresh food, health services, education, and childcare are among these goods and services tax exemptions.

How to Calculate GST manually-

  • (i) To add indirect taxes to a price (calculating GST-inclusive price)
  • (ii) GST-Inclusive Price = Original Price × (1 + GST Rate)
  • Example (Australia, 10% GST): $100 × 1.10 = $110
  • (iii) To calculate extract indirect taxes from a inclusive price (reverse calculation)
  • GST Amount = GST-Inclusive Price ÷ (1 + GST Rate) × GST Rate
  • Example (NZ, 15% GST): $115 ÷ 1.15 × 0.15 = $15 GST
​

To calculate indirect tax in India (e.g., 18%):

GST Amount = Original Price × 18 ÷ 100 Example: ₹1,000 × 0.18 = ₹180 GST making the total ₹1,180.
